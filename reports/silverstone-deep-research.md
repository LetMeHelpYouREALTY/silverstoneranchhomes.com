## Silverstone Ranch Community Overview

Silverstone Ranch is a **1,500+-home master-planned community** in northwest Las Vegas, situated within the Centennial Hills area in the **89131 zip code**. Developed by Pulte Homes between 2003 and 2008, the community sits at the base of the Sheep Mountain range, approximately 25 minutes from the Las Vegas Strip. When originally built, many homes surrounded a **270-acre, 27-hole golf course** - the Silverstone Golf Club - which has been closed since 2015 [11]. The community anchors the northern edge of the Centennial Hills master plan and benefits from the broader area's rapid expansion, including new retail, medical, and transportation infrastructure along the 215 Beltway corridor [2].

The community comprises **eleven distinct enclaves** across two security tiers: three gated neighborhoods (with one guard-gated) and six non-gated villages, plus additional sub-neighborhoods such as Tuscany and Pinehurst within the gated tier. Total home counts exceed **1,526** across all enclaves [12].

## Enclave-by-Enclave Profiles

### The Palms (Guard-Gated)

The Palms is the **only guard-gated enclave** in Silverstone Ranch, featuring 24/7 manned guard-house entry. Homes range from **1,900 to 4,050 square feet** with 3 to 5 bedrooms and 2 to 3-car garages. Select lots reach up to **one-third acre**, making these among the largest in the community. Built between 2004 and 2009, The Palms offers the widest architectural variety and lot sizes. Monthly HOA totals **$240** ($158 master + $82 sub), covering guard-house staffing, boulevard landscaping, trail lighting, and a private park [11]; [21].

### Silverlake (Gated)

Silverlake is a **gated enclave** featuring single-story homes ranging from **2,025 to 2,825 square feet** with 3 to 4 bedrooms and 2 to 3-car garages. Lot sizes range from approximately 7,000 sq ft to one-quarter acre. Homes were built between 2004 and 2006 [11]. HOA fees fall in the gated-enclave tier.

### Pinehurst (Gated - Golf Corridor)

Pinehurst comprises **single and two-story townhomes** ranging from **1,345 to 2,015 square feet** with 2 to 4 bedrooms and attached 2-car garages. Located adjacent to the former Silverstone Golf Club, Pinehurst carries the **highest total HOA at $286/mo** ($158 master + $128 sub), covering roof and exterior insurance, landscape, a private pool, and pest control [11]; [21]. Built 2004-2006.

### Tuscany and Cottages (Gated Single-Family)

Tuscany and its Cottages sub-collection form a **gated single-family enclave** with total HOA of **$252/mo** ($158 master + $94 sub). Fees cover front-yard landscape maintenance, an exterior paint cycle, clubhouse access, fitness center, and reserves [21]. This enclave provides a middle ground between the luxury Palms product and the non-gated villages.

### Windermere (Gated)

Windermere is the **third gated enclave** in Silverstone Ranch. According to a Silverstone Ranch community Facebook post, the HOA board intends to raise all homeowners' dues in 2026, with **Windermere owners facing a second consecutive dues increase** Silverstone Ranch Facebook. Buyers should verify current and proposed dues before contracting. HOA fees are expected to be in the gated tier, comparable to The Palms or Tuscany.

### Eastpoint, Greenfield, Somerset (Non-Gated Villages)

These three non-gated enclaves share a common sub-HOA structure at **$200/mo total** ($158 master + $42 sub), the lowest in Silverstone Ranch. ## HOA Fee Structure and 2026 Dues Outlook

Every Silverstone Ranch homeowner pays a **master HOA of $158/month**, which funds community-wide infrastructure: arterial road landscaping, the central park system, trail network lighting, and community-wide security patrols. Individual enclaves levy an additional sub-HOA ranging from **$42 to $128/month**, resulting in total monthly obligations between **$200 and $286** [21].

FirstService Residential, Nevada's leading association management company, was awarded the community management contract for Silverstone Ranch, bringing professional oversight to reserve planning and vendor management [41].

**2026 Dues Increase Alert**: A community Facebook post from the Silverstone Ranch HOA page indicates the board intends to **increase all homeowners' dues in 2026**, with Windermere owners specifically facing a **second consecutive dues increase** that would push their monthly costs higher Silverstone Ranch Facebook. The post also references over $762MM collectively in the community's banking reserves, with residents questioning the claimed financial need for increases. Buyers should request the most recent HOA budget and reserve study before committing.

## Silverstone Golf Club: Closure, Auction, and Redevelopment Uncertainty

The Silverstone Golf Club - once a **270-acre, 27-hole championship course** - has been **closed since September 2, 2015**. The closure followed a sale in which the new owner immediately shut down operations; a September 1 letter from the seller confirmed the closure [19]; [16].

On **May 8, 2025**, the property was sold at auction for approximately **$2.8 million**, despite more than **$12 million in recorded liens** against it. The buyer's identity will be released upon deed recording, typically within 30 days of sale. As of November 2025, **no redevelopment plan has been announced** [20].

Any redevelopment of the golf course land would require **City of Las Vegas entitlements** and **75% homeowner approval**, creating a high threshold for any land-use change. The clubhouse site has been **vacant since a fire in November 2021** [20].

**Impact on Property Values**: Fairway-adjacent lots currently carry a **6% to 10% discount** compared to 2014 pricing levels. Appraisals should be based on comparable sales without assuming an operating golf course. Per Nevada law, sellers must disclose the auction sale, the ongoing closure, and the absence of redevelopment plans to prospective buyers [20].

**Buyer Guidance**: Buyers should not price properties assuming the course will reopen, should budget for private landscaping if their lot faces the dormant fairways, and should treat any future course reopening as speculative upside rather than a baseline assumption.

## Synthesis: Cross-Cutting Insights and Strategic Implications

Three non-obvious tensions define the Silverstone Ranch investment landscape in mid-2026:

**Tension 1 - Price Resilience vs. Amenity Erosion**: The community's median sale price has risen 11.1% year-over-year to $500K despite a decade-long golf course closure and a burned-out clubhouse. This resilience reflects the guard-gated premium (The Palms) and Centennial Hills' overall desirability, not the golf-adjacent lifestyle that originally drove premium pricing. The mechanism: buyer demand for gated security and larger lots in Northwest Las Vegas outweighs the negative amenity signal. Implication: if the golf course reopens or is redeveloped into a park or residential use, prices could see an additional upside catalyst; if the land remains dormant indefinitely, the current discount may widen as aging infrastructure compounds the visual blight.

**Tension 2 - HOA Cost Escalation vs. Service Delivery**: Residents are questioning 2026 dues increases given reported reserves exceeding $762MM across the community's banking, yet the HOA claims financial strain. Windermere owners face a second consecutive increase. The mechanism: reserve requirements for aging infrastructure (20-year-old homes approaching major component replacement cycles) and golf-course-adjacent land maintenance obligations are driving budget pressure even as cash reserves appear healthy. Implication: buyers should request full reserve study documentation; escalating HOA costs could compress affordability for non-gated village buyers who chose Silverstone Ranch for its $200/mo entry point.

**Tension 3 - Enclave Segmentation vs. Community Cohesion**: The eleven enclaves span a wide value spectrum (Pinehurst townhomes at ~$300K to Palms estates approaching $700K+), creating fundamentally different buyer profiles within one master plan. The mechanism: the master HOA structure forces shared cost allocation across enclaves with divergent service expectations (guard-gated residents expect 24/7 security; non-gated residents prioritize lower dues). Implication: any HOA governance dispute that pits enclave interests against each other could affect community perception and, by extension, property values; REALTORS should screen buyer clients for their enclave-culture fit as carefully as for home features.

The convergence of these tensions places Silverstone Ranch at an inflection point: a well-located, architecturally diverse community with proven price resilience, facing headwinds from unresolved golf course land, rising HOA costs, and a broadening market that gives buyers increasing alternatives.
